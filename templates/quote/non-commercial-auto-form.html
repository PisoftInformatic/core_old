Non Commercial Auto Insurance

Auto insurance is an essential safeguard that one must acquire when owning an auto or any other type of vehicle. Its purpose is to provide protection in the event of an accident. Auto insurance offers various forms of protection, such as covering the expenses for repairing your vehicle after an accident and addressing liability claims if you are found responsible for causing damage.

Insurance companies often categorize auto insurance as property and casualty insurance, which also encompasses home insurance, business insurance, and disaster insurance.

It is important to note that if an individual who is not listed on your policy has an accident with your auto, it may result in an increase in your own insurance premiums.

Auto Insurance Policy Coverage:

Your insurance company will provide you with coverage, which refers to the maximum amount of money they will pay you in the event of a claim. It is advisable to inquire about the specific coverage provided by your policy from your insurance company.

  • Liability insurance : provides coverage for any losses incurred due to injury or death caused by your vehicle to other individuals. Additionally, it offers protection for any damage caused by your vehicle to other vehicles. However, it is important to note that if the cost of the loss or damage exceeds your liability limit, you may be required to personally cover the remaining balance of the settlement. It is crucial to understand that liability insurance does not include coverage for repairs to your own vehicle. Therefore, it is advisable to explore additional insurance options to ensure adequate coverage for such costs.
  • Accident benefits :encompass the expenses incurred for personal medical treatment and income loss resulting from a motor vehicle collision. Your auto/vehicle insurance policy may also offer you supplementary advantages.
  • Collision insurance : provides coverage for the expenses associated with repairing or replacing your vehicle in the event of a collision with another vehicle or object. It is often included as part of your compulsory insurance coverage by insurance companies.
  • Comprehensive insurance : provides coverage for the expenses incurred in repairing or replacing your vehicle as a result of various forms of damage or loss. This encompasses incidents such as vandalism, damage to your vehicle's windshield, and theft. However, it's important to note that comprehensive insurance does not extend coverage to specific types of loss or damage, such as collisions with other vehicles or objects.
